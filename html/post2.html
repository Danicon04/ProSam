CHORD INVERSIONS

Chord inversions simply means changing the position of notes in a chord Eg. Cmaj is C E G, this is called the root position. This chord can have 2 inversions. For the 1st inversion, we'll take the lowest note C and place it ahead of G, then we'll have the 1st inversion as: E G C. For the 2nd inversion, we'll take the lowest note of your 1st inversion which is E and place it ahead of C, it becomes: G C E. It works for any kind of chord, we're simply repositioning the notes in a systematic order… Finally, the number of chord inversions a chord can have depends on the number of notes the chord contains... This means that a: 3 note chord has 1st and 2nd inversions 4 note chord will have 1st, 2nd and 3rd inversions 5 note chord has 1st, 2nd, 3rd and 4th inversions And so on...

DIMINISHED CHORDS
Diminished chords consists of the following notes - Root + min3rd + dim5th... Eg.
Cdim - CEbGb
Ebdim - Eb Gb Bbb…

MODULATION
Modulation is the act of changing the pitch within a music piece, and oftentimes coming back to the original key. So for instance, if you were singing or playing a song on the keyboard and you had to change the key in the middle of the song then we'll say you modulated. To be able understand these properly you must take a proper piano course and this will save you a lot of time and help you avoid unnecessary mistakes...
