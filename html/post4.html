MUSIC CAREER IN CHURCH - POSSIBLE OR NOT

There is a career part of the gospel music industry! There is no debating it. While we debunk the excesses of gospel singers, we can't deny the financial realities. Music is expensive, very! From studio production to marketing to video shoots, the whole way down! You can't possibly expect singers to spend huge resources making music and they won't charge. I mean, there is a reason Pastors sell our books, yeah? There is a reason some pastors even charge for folks to access their messages. And yep, we all have church staff that are paid, some heavily paid. You can't just claim that the fellow servicing the generator is serving God and shouldn't be paid or the fellow who handles the precious gadgets we bought with millions. These are the realities. Yep, the singers may have become unruly and ambitious or even worldly but we can't just ignore the facts. Meanwhile, we were also wrong to assume anyone who has a gospel music career is a minister in God's house. They are not like choir members who seek no financial gain or renumeration from their service in God's house. These people are career folks and we better settle with that reality. No need mixing it up. No need at all. We have to be comfortable with them having their career without assuming they are part of our choir. That's the fact. We should be more concerned about their worldliness than trying to de-emphasize their career or role. There will always be need for gospel music. We will always need the gospel alternative to worldly songs. Or would you suggest gospel drama outlets should also close up and they shouldn't sell their movies or generate monies from it? Or maybe folks should stop selling bibles because it is God's word. Or folks who make gospel cartoon for kids don't need to monetise that because it is gospel. A fellow was even angry gospel artists actually have copyright laws guiding the use of the work, he suppose it shouldn't be so, since it is gospel music. Interesting, because same person will be angry to see another Pastor plagarise his own pastor's Facebook posts. Let's keep a cerebral perspective while we discuss these issues. You know I am never one to lose my intelligence while reasoning doctrinally, intelligence is a gift, not a curse, as long as it is submissive to God's word. I always wonder why we always run into extremes in a bid to correct extremes, we just like to destroy everything!! Can we really say no good has come out of the numerous gospel art in Nigeria? Huh? No good? All of a sudden the HolySpirit is angry and wants us to discard it all together? Nah, I think we are overreacting. It is not that deep!

Rev'd Harry
